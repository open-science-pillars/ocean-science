# AMO / AMV index: North Atlantic SST anomaly and the detrending choice

The Atlantic Multidecadal Oscillation (also AMV) is a North Atlantic
(commonly 0-60N) mean SST anomaly. The detrending choice is the
scientific controversy at the center of the definition: linear
detrending, global-mean removal, and forced-signal regression yield
visibly different decadal narratives. There is no neutral choice.

**Practice.** Name the detrending choice, and for any conclusion that
depends on it, show at least one alternative detrending (the
sensitivity discipline mirrors the mixed-layer criterion-sensitivity
practice). An AMO conclusion presented without naming its detrending
choice is not reproducible, because the choice, not the data, drives
the decadal story.
